Proposal to Establish a Cricket Club in Ghent

For some time, we have been discussing the possibility of establishing a formal cricket club in Ghent.

Starting an official cricket club could provide better access to sports facilities, insurance coverage for registered members, possible financial support, and a more stable structure for organizing the club.

At the same time, a formal club requires administration, financial management, regular reporting, coordination, and several members willing to invest time in managing the organization.

Recently, Kural and Prem discussed this possibility with representatives of the Sportdienst of the City of Ghent. This page summarizes the main advantages, requirements, responsibilities, and practical considerations.

1. Why establish a formal club?

A formal club structure may allow the group to work under a recognized organization and manage cricket activities more transparently.

Operate under an official name and open a club bank account.
Collect and manage membership fees transparently.
Register members formally and arrange insurance.
Join an appropriate federation and apply for City of Ghent recognition.
Access possible reduced facility rates, subsidies, or support.
Create a stable organizational structure.

These benefits depend on the rules, approvals, and conditions of the relevant authorities.

2. VZW structure

The proposed club would be established as a Belgian non-profit association, or VZW. A VZW gives the club a formal legal structure, but it also brings obligations.

  • • Formal statutes and founding decision
  • • Board and official registered address
  • • Proper financial administration
  • • Annual reporting and filing deadlines
  • • Compliance with applicable VZW obligations
  • • Tax and reporting duties depending on circumstances

3. Board and organizing team

At least three committed people are expected to be needed for the initial board or leadership structure, subject to the final statutes and legal advice.

Responsibilities should be divided across several people. The club should not depend on only one or two organizers.

Chairperson Secretary Treasurer Membership Insurance Facilities Website Subsidies

4. Official registered address

The VZW needs an official registered address in Belgium. The person providing it should have a stable address, be actively involved, and understand that official correspondence may be sent there.

Home ownership, Belgian citizenship, or permanent residence should not be presented as automatic legal requirements.

5. Statutes and internal rules

The statutes should cover the club name and purpose, membership conditions, fees, member rights, board appointment/removal, voting, meetings, finances, amendments, and dissolution.

Additional internal rules can cover practical club administration.

6. Membership registration

A person becomes an official member only after completing registration, providing required information, paying the applicable fee, and where relevant being registered with the federation.

Formal registration is needed for insurance, membership administration, voting rights, federation registration, financial planning, and official club records.

7. Membership-payment options

Annual membership

Recommended because it reduces administration, gives stable income, supports budgeting, and simplifies insurance/federation registration.

Monthly membership

More flexible for members, but it requires more payment monitoring and administration.

Per game or session

May suit occasional participants, but it creates significantly more administration and makes annual planning harder.

No final membership amount is set. It can be determined only after calculating insurance, federation, venue, equipment, banking, website, and administrative costs.

8. Member insurance

Registered members may be covered through the club’s federation or sports-insurance policy. Cricket Vlaanderen currently describes collective civil-liability and sports-accident coverage for properly registered and licensed members of affiliated clubs, including relevant training sessions, matches, and travel, subject to the policy conditions.

  • • Coverage applies only when registration and payment conditions are completed.
  • • Exact coverage depends on the federation and insurance policy.
  • • The club must confirm the final premium and accident-reporting procedure.
  • • The insurer decides whether a claim is covered.

9. Possible reimbursement through your health-insurance fund

Some Belgian health-insurance funds provide their members with a partial annual reimbursement of sports-club membership fees.

After paying the club membership fee, an eligible member may ask the club to complete or sign the reimbursement form from their mutuality, or provide a membership certificate with the required information.

  • • Member name and club name
  • • Sport practised and season period
  • • Amount paid and payment date
  • • Club contact details and authorized signature/stamp

The member submits the document to their own mutuality. Amounts and eligibility vary, so reimbursement is not guaranteed by the club.

Request a membership reimbursement certificate

10. Sports facilities and possible support

Formal recognition may provide access to reduced tariffs or possible subsidies. Exact rental reductions depend on the facility and applicable regulations.

The previously discussed reduction of up to approximately 80% must be confirmed. Reimbursement for alternative facilities is not automatic and may require proof and prior authorization.

11. Financial administration

The club would need a VZW bank account, accurate income and expense records, invoices, receipts, a membership-payment register, annual budget, annual accounts or financial report, member approval where required, and timely filings.

Missed deadlines can lead to penalties or administrative consequences.

12. Main responsibilities

Governance

Board meetings, member meetings, minutes, statutes, legal deadlines, and communication with authorities.

Finance

Budget, bank account, records, invoices, reporting, tax-related filing, and subsidy applications.

Membership

Registration, records, fee collection, federation registration, renewals, and certificates.

Insurance

Insurance registration, records, accident forms, and communication with the federation or insurer.

Facility booking

Suitable halls and grounds, reservations, deadlines, payments, and provider communication.

Website and communication

Official information, forms, member communication, privacy, and data-management checks.

Equipment

Purchasing, inventory, storage, maintenance, and transport.
